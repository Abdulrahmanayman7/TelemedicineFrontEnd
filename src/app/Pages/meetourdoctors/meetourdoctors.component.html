Professional Highlights

Dr. Mohamed Mostafa graduated from the Faculty of Medicine at Kuwait University in 1992. He then moved directly to Jordan to continue his specialization in surgery. In 1997 he received a fellowship from the Royal College of Surgeons of Ireland. In 1999, he received a degree of specialization in general surgery at the University Hospital of Jordan and then the Jordanian Board of General Surgery. During that time, he began his interest in oncology surgery and began work in the Oncology Department at the University of Jordan Hospital for two years. During this period he gained experience in surgery on many tumors, and his interest began to increase in this field. So, in 2002, he traveled to Dublin, Ireland, to pursue his oncology training, especially breast tumors, thyroid, colorectal tumors, as well as training in laparoscopic surgery. In 2005, he returned to Jordan after completing his training. He immediately joined the King Hussein Cancer Center as a General Surgery and Tumor Surgery Specialist and as Associate Professor of the Center for Medical Education (PEC) of the Center. He participated in many high-level medical education activities for graduates, residents and colleagues. Skills to be able to provide the best medical services to patients. During this period, he conducted numerous researches in the field of surgery and tumors, published in several international medical journals.
